CktGen: Automated Analog Circuit Design with Generative Artificial Intelligence

CktGen reframes analog circuit synthesis as specification-conditioned generation. Instead of re-optimizing each new target from scratch, it learns to generate candidate circuits directly from desired specifications and then improves them at test time.

Yuxuan Hou, Hehe Fan, Jianrong Zhang, Yue Zhang, Hua Chen, Min Zhou, Faxin Yu, Roger Zimmermann, Yi Yang
Published in Engineering (中国工程院院刊), 2025

Why this matters

From fixed-target optimization to specification-driven generation

Real analog design rarely stays fixed. Specifications change across applications, and one valid target can map to multiple circuit realizations. CktGen is built for that regime.

Overview

What CktGen changes

Most prior methods treat analog circuit synthesis as optimization under fixed objectives. CktGen instead asks a more flexible question: given target specifications, can we directly generate valid circuit candidates and continue optimizing them without retraining?

01

Problem shift

Design targets such as gain, bandwidth, and phase margin vary across scenarios. CktGen reformulates synthesis as a specification-conditioned generation task rather than a single-objective search problem.

02

One-to-many generation

A single target specification can correspond to multiple valid implementations. The model learns a joint latent space that preserves this diversity instead of collapsing everything into one deterministic answer.

03

Generation plus optimization

CktGen first generates circuits from specifications, then performs test-time optimization with a multi-armed bandit strategy to search for higher-quality candidates under explicit constraints.

Figure 1. Overall framework of CktGen. The upper half learns a shared latent space across specifications and circuits. The lower half performs test-time optimization toward target specification thresholds.

At a glance

Why optimization alone is not enough

In practical analog design, the bottleneck is not only how fast a method can optimize, but whether it can adapt when the target specification changes. Existing methods often require extra search, manual redesign, or even retraining when facing new combinations of constraints.

CktGen is designed for a more realistic regime: specification changes are expected, and multiple circuit realizations may satisfy the same target. That is why the method is built around cross-modal alignment and controllable generation rather than around a single fixed objective.

Flexible targets

Works with changing specifications instead of assuming one static design goal.

Structured latent space

Aligns circuit representations and specification representations in a shared space.

Practical deployment

Supports search and refinement at inference time without training a new model.

Method

A pipeline built for controllable analog circuit generation

The model combines circuit encoding, specification encoding, latent alignment, and autoregressive decoding. The key objective is not just to generate plausible circuits, but to make generated circuits stay consistent with the requested specifications.

1

Dual encoders

A Transformer-based VAE encodes circuit structure, while an MLP encodes target specifications. Both branches map into a shared latent representation.

2

Triple alignment

Contrastive learning, classifier guidance, and feature alignment work together to organize the latent space so that paired circuits and specifications stay coherent while diversity is preserved.

3

Autoregressive generation

A GPT-style decoder generates node types, topology, device parameters, and edges step by step, turning specification embeddings into concrete circuit candidates.

Figure 2. CktGen architecture. The model contains a circuit encoder, a specification encoder, a shared latent alignment module, and an autoregressive decoder for circuit generation.

Results

The key question is not whether circuits look valid, but whether they match the target

CktGen is evaluated on Ckt-Bench-101 and Ckt-Bench-301 from the Open Circuit Benchmark. The main signal is specification accuracy: whether the generated circuit actually satisfies the requested performance target.

47.57% Spec-Acc on Ckt-Bench-101, while all baselines stay below 3%.
22.64% Spec-Acc on Ckt-Bench-301 with strong FID and structural validity.

Tables 2 and 3. Quantitative results on specification-conditioned generation. CktGen substantially improves specification accuracy while keeping strong generation quality and structural validity.

Why the latent space matters

The learned latent space is not just a hidden representation. It is the core mechanism that makes specification-conditioned generation practical. CktGen forms compact within-class clusters and clearer between-class separation, which supports controllable retrieval, generation, and optimization.

Figure 3. t-SNE visualization of latent space representations. CktGen produces cleaner class separation and tighter grouping than the compared baselines.

Automated Design

From generation to practical target-driven design

Beyond conditional generation, CktGen addresses a more realistic setting: given target thresholds such as gain, bandwidth, and phase margin, generate circuits that satisfy all constraints and continue searching for higher FoM designs.

At inference time, CktGen first identifies feasible specification regions in the latent space. If the requested target falls outside the supported distribution, the framework explicitly treats it as out of domain rather than returning misleading candidates.

For valid targets, a multi-armed bandit strategy explores candidate regions and pushes the search toward stronger designs without retraining the model. This extends CktGen from a generator into a design tool.

87.09% Spec-Acc on Ckt-Bench-101 85.07% Spec-Acc on Ckt-Bench-301 No retraining during optimization

Figure 4. Automated design results under target constraints. CktGen reaches the highest specification accuracy on both datasets while keeping competitive FoM.

Takeaway

A new framing for analog circuit synthesis

CktGen shows that analog circuit design can be approached as specification-conditioned generation rather than repeated fixed-target optimization. By aligning specifications and circuits in a shared latent space, it supports diverse generation, target-aware decoding, and test-time optimization in a unified pipeline.
